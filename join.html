Opportunities for doctoral researchers, project students, visitors and collaborators in pathogen genomics and One Health.

06

A good fit

Curiosity about biology matters more than collecting methods.

Projects are built around a clear biological or public-health question. Genomics, epidemiology, metagenomics and machine learning are tools chosen because they help answer that question.

Researchers are expected to work reproducibly, communicate uncertainty honestly and treat international collaborators as intellectual partners.

Current themes

  • Transmission across human, animal and environmental systems
  • Bacterial evolution, recombination and host adaptation
  • Open genomic surveillance and population annotation
  • Genomics and AI for predicting source, resistance and disease
06A

Routes into the group

Different stages, shared expectations.

DPhil / PhD

Multi-paper projects with a connected thesis question, explicit skills plan and meaningful participation in collaborative networks.

MSc and rotations

Focused projects designed around one answerable question and a realistic route to a complete analysis.

Visiting researchers

Short placements linked to defined datasets, methods, training or manuscript development.

Collaborations

Partnerships that combine complementary data, expertise and local knowledge rather than simply exchanging samples.

First contact

What to include in an email.

  1. The scientific question that interests you
  2. Your relevant research or analytical experience
  3. The degree, fellowship or funding route you are considering
  4. Why the Pascoe Lab and its collaborators are a good fit
  5. A concise CV or profile link

There may not always be an advertised funded position. Formal opportunities will be linked here when available.
