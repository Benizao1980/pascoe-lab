Project 02-A

Enteric disease in Africa.

GETcampy and the Campylobacter Control Campaign connect One Health sampling, genomic epidemiology, modelling and prevention.

A

Project passport

Understanding exposure in connected human, animal and environmental systems.

The programme studies childhood enteric disease alongside asymptomatic carriage and relevant household, livestock, food, water and environmental sources.

GETcampy established coordinated protocols and genomic sampling. CCC expands that foundation towards population annotation, source attribution, intervention modelling, training and prevention.

A1

Study system

Sampling the routes through which children encounter enteric bacteria.

Children

Diarrhoeal disease, asymptomatic carriage and repeated infection during early life.

Households

Family members, surfaces, food preparation and shared environmental exposures.

Animals and food

Poultry and livestock populations embedded in local production and household systems.

Water and environment

Potential routes that connect otherwise separate host populations.

Leadership and funding

Built through successive collaborative programmes.

Open infrastructure

Protocols and population annotation are research outputs.
