Ben Pascoe · Global pathogen genomics & evolution

Advancing pathogen genomics, antimicrobial resistance and One Health

We explore how bacterial pathogens evolve, spread and cause disease, combining genomic epidemiology, machine learning and international collaboration to improve public health.

Research themes

From genomes to public-health action

Global pathogen genomics

Understanding disease transmission and persistence in settings where genomic and epidemiological data remain scarce, especially across South America, West Africa and Southeast Asia.

Evolution and spread of AMR

Investigating how resistance mutations, genes and mobile elements emerge and move through bacterial populations, livestock systems and the food chain.

Predicting disease outcomes

Using large-scale microbial genomics and machine learning to identify lineages and genetic traits associated with severe disease and post-infection complications.

Current research highlights

Campylobacter · AMR · Peru

Emerging multidrug-resistant Campylobacter in LMICs

Research into the sharing of cmeRABC alleles between C. coli and C. jejuni, and the emergence of multidrug-resistant lineages in the Peruvian Amazon.

Machine learning · Source attribution

Attributing the source of Campylobacter infections

Machine-learning analysis of national surveillance genomes to estimate the livestock and environmental origins of human infection.

Child health · Global epidemiology

Asymptomatic and persistent Campylobacter infection

Investigating the epidemiology of persistent infection and its consequences for childhood growth in resource-limited settings.

One Health · Wildlife

Zoonotic transfer of antimicrobial resistance

Studying how proximity to people and agriculture shapes antimicrobial-resistant enteric pathogens in wild-bird microbiomes.
