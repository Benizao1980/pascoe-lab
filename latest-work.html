Global pathogen genomics

Our research addresses diseases that disproportionately affect low- and middle-income countries. We combine microbiology, genomics and systems biology to understand how pathogens spread, persist and cause harm, working with collaborators across South America, West Africa and Southeast Asia.

The evolution and spread of AMR

We study how resistance evolves through mutation, recombination and horizontal gene transfer. Current work focuses particularly on Campylobacter and Salmonella, including resistance to macrolides, fluoroquinolones and other clinically important drugs.

Predicting disease outcomes from microbial genomes

Large genomic datasets can reveal bacterial traits associated with severe disease. We develop scalable analytical approaches and help build genomic capacity internationally, with work spanning gastrointestinal cancer, irritable bowel syndrome and Guillain–Barré syndrome.
